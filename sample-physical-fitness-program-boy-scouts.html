    The Various Types of Boy Scout Physical Forms

    There are many different types of these forms that allow schools to figure out if certain Boy Scouts are up to certain tasks that require certain physical conditions to be met. Here are some examples of these types of forms:

    • Boy Scout Sports Physical Forms are the kind of forms that are used by those people who need to know if these young men are ready to participate in certain sports that are held during Boy Scout events, like jamborees for example.
    • Boy Scout Doctor Physical Forms are for those who need to have an actual doctor check their physical condition. Some Boy Scout organizations require an actual doctor’s approval before they can allow their scouts to participate in certain rigorous tasks.
    • Boy Scout Annual Physical Forms are mainly used for when Boy Scouts need to have their annual physical check-up. These are used to compare a young man’s current physical status with his status from the previous year. These forms are usually employed to see if that scout is still fit to carry on duty or if it’s time for him to retire from the program.
